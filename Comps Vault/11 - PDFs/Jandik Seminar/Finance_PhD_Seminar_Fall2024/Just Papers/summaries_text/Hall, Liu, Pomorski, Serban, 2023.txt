**Summary:**  
The paper introduces a novel metric for assessing supply chain climate exposure, emphasizing that a company's climate risk extends beyond its direct emissions (scope 1 and 2) to include the carbon intensity of its customers and suppliers. The downstream exposure measure calculates a revenue-weighted average of customers’ scope 1+2 emissions intensity, capturing risks from climate-related disruptions affecting key clients. Empirical validation shows this measure correlates with stock price movements around climate news (e.g., +5% outperformance by "green" stocks post-G8 emissions pledge, -2% underperformance post-2016 U.S. election) and exhibits stronger alignment with market sentiment (correlation ~0.2) than traditional emissions data. A long-short portfolio based on the metric achieved a Sharpe ratio of 0.85, with returns not fully explained by standard factor models. The measure also predicts earnings and sales surprises, indicating its relevance to fundamentals. Unlike scope 3 emissions, which focus on product lifecycles, this approach prioritizes economic linkages and uses more accessible data, making it practical for risk management and ESG integration.

**Key Takeaways:**  
- **Strong Performance Signal:** Portfolios long low-exposure ("green") and short high-exposure ("brown") stocks generated a Sharpe ratio of **0.85** (0.72 industry-neutral), with annualized alphas of **6.5–9%** against factor models.  
- **Climate News Sensitivity:** The measure explains stock reactions to climate events, with green stocks outperforming brown peers by **~5%** after positive climate news and underperforming by **~2%** after negative events.  
- **Fundamental Predictive Power:** Downstream exposure predicts quarterly EPS surprises (**+9.3%** unadjusted, **+3.8%** industry-adjusted), highlighting its utility beyond traditional quality or momentum metrics.
