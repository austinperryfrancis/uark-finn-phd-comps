**Summary**  
This study employs machine learning to quantify corporate culture using earnings call transcripts. The authors trained a word embedding model (word2vec) on 209,480 transcripts (2001–2018) to create a "culture dictionary" for five values: innovation, integrity, quality, respect, and teamwork. They found that an innovative culture extends beyond R&D and patents, encapsulating broader strategic and operational practices. Corporate culture correlates with key outcomes: firms with strong cultures exhibit higher operational efficiency (assets turnover +0.052***), risk-taking (stock volatility +0.005***), and firm value (Tobin’s q +0.043***), while engaging in less earnings management (discretionary accruals -0.917**). The culture-performance link intensifies during crises, such as the 2008 financial crisis (+2.4% monthly returns for resilient firms). Mergers and acquisitions (M&As) both reflect and reshape culture: culturally aligned firms are more likely to merge, and post-M&A, acquirers’ cultural scores adapt to their targets’ pre-merger values.

**Key Takeaways**  
- **Innovation Beyond R&D**: An innovative culture (coefficient 0.098*** for patent impact) encompasses strategic agility and operational practices, not just R&D or patents.  
- **Culture Drives Performance**: Strong cultures correlate with efficiency, risk-taking, and valuation (e.g., Tobin’s q +0.043***) and reduce earnings manipulation (discretionary accruals -1.150**).  
- **M&As and Cultural Dynamics**: Culturally similar firms are likelier to merge (cultural similarity coefficient 4.305***). Post-M&A, 70–80% of acquirers’ cultural values align with targets’, indicating mutual acculturation.
