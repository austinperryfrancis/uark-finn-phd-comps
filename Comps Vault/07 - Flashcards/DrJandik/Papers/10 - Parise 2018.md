## Front
Parise (2018)
Threat of entry and debt maturity: Evidence from airlines

## Back

### Research Question
Do firms adjust debt maturity in anticipation of future product-market competition?

### Construct
Threat of entry = share of an airline’s routes threatened by likely low-cost-carrier entry, weighted by passenger traffic; debt maturity = share of debt maturing in more than three years.

### Data
U.S. domestic airlines; DOT route/passenger data linked to airline financials and debt issuance data.

### Design/ID
Uses low-cost carriers’ entry into the second endpoint airport of a route to predict likely future route entry; regresses airline debt maturity/leverage on route-weighted entry threat with airline and year fixed effects.

### Findings
Threatened airlines increase debt maturity before entry occurs, especially speculative-grade/constrained airlines; a one-SD increase in threat raises long-term debt share by about 6 percentage points. Threat does not significantly affect leverage; threatened firms mainly reduce rollover risk by extending maturity.
