# Massa et al. (2022)

Paper: [[Massa_Zhang_2022]]
Week: 7 - Networks, internal capital markets, and diversification

## Front
Massa et al. (2022)
International asset pricing with strategic business groups

## Back

### Research Question
Do strategic business groups affect international asset pricing by reallocating risk/resources across affiliated firms?

### Construct
Business-group centrality = how important an affiliated firm is for maintaining control of the overall business group; central firms are core firms whose failure would threaten group control.

### Data
International publicly listed firms affiliated with business groups; worldwide ownership data from Orbis linked to accounting and stock-return data.

### Design/ID
Constructs firm centrality within business-group ownership networks; tests whether central firms are protected in bad times using negative industry shocks, commodity shocks, and sovereign downgrade shocks; then tests whether centrality predicts expected returns and improves international asset-pricing models.

### Findings
Business groups protect central firms in bad times by reallocating risk/resources within the group. Central firms are less exposed to negative shocks and earn lower expected returns. A central-minus-peripheral factor helps explain international stock returns, suggesting strategic business groups create an additional asset-pricing factor outside standard U.S.-style models.
