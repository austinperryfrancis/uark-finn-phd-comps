# Hall et al. (2023)

Paper: [[Hall, Liu, Pomorski, and Serban 2023]]
Week: 12 - Climate finance

## Front
Hall et al. (2023)
Supply Chain Climate Exposure

## Back

### Research Question
Can firms have material climate risk exposure through supply-chain relationships, even when their own emissions look low?

### Construct
Supply-chain climate exposure = business-weighted exposure to the climate risks of a firm’s customers or suppliers; downstream exposure is measured as a revenue-weighted average of customers’ carbon intensity.

### Data
Global public firms with supply-chain relationship data linked to firm-level carbon/emissions measures, climate-news sentiment, stock returns, and fundamentals.

### Design/ID
Constructs supply-chain climate exposure measures and validates whether they explain price reactions to climate news and performance patterns beyond traditional emissions measures.

### Findings
Supply-chain climate exposure captures indirect climate risk not reflected in a firm’s own emissions. Firms connected to carbon-intensive customers or suppliers can have high climate exposure, and the measure better captures climate-news repricing than standard emissions metrics.
