# Cai et al. (2022)

Paper: [[Cai_Nguyen_Walkling_2022]]
Week: 6 - Networks in corporate finance

## Front
Cai et al. (2022)
Director Appointments: It Is Who You Know

## Back

### Research Question
How important are professional connections in director appointments, and when do connected director appointments help or hurt shareholder value?

### Construct
Connected director = new director with first- or second-degree professional ties to the incumbent board; connection source = incumbent directors versus CEO.

### Data
U.S. director appointments from BoardEx linked to firm characteristics, announcement returns, shareholder voting, and director-network data.

### Design/ID
Tests whether connected candidates are more likely to be appointed and how markets/shareholders react; uses death-induced network losses and merger-induced network gains as instruments for the availability of connected candidates.

### Findings
Most new directors are professionally connected to incumbent boards, and connections help firms appoint directors with new skills, industry backgrounds, and gender diversity. Connected appointments are valued more when coordination/search benefits are high, such as in complex or competitive firms. But CEO-connected directors receive lower announcement returns and shareholder votes, consistent with agency/cronyism concerns.
