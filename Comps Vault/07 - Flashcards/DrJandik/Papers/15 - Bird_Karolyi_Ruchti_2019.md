# Bird et al. (2019)

Paper: [[Bird_Karolyi_Ruchti_2019]]
Week: 4 - Debt contracts: covenants, social capital, lender hold-up, and creditor control

## Front
Bird et al. (2019)
Information Sharing, Holdup, and External Finance: Evidence from Private Firms

## Back

### Research Question
Do private borrowers voluntarily share information to reduce incumbent-lender hold-up and improve access to external finance?

### Construct
Information sharing = voluntary disclosure of private financial measures in loan documentation; lender hold-up = incumbent lender’s information advantage over outside lenders.

### Data
Private-firm loans from LPC/DealScan; loan disclosures, lender relationships, lender switching, spreads, loan amounts, and IPO subsample.

### Design/ID
Regresses lender competition and loan terms on borrower information sharing with borrower/lender controls and FE; uses firms approaching IPOs and time-to-IPO IV to address unobserved borrower quality.

### Findings
Only a minority of private borrowers disclose, but disclosers switch lenders more often, borrow from larger syndicates, pay lower spreads, and receive larger loans. Voluntary information sharing reduces lender hold-up by increasing outside-lender competition and easing financing constraints.
