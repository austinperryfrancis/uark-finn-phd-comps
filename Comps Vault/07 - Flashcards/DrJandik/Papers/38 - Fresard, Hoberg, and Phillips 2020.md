# Fresard et al. (2020)

Paper: [[Fresard, Hoberg, and Phillips 2020]]
Week: 10 - M&A: sources of synergies

## Front
Fresard et al. (2020)
Innovation Activities and Integration through Vertical Acquisitions

## Back

### Research Question
How does the stage of innovation affect firms’ vertical acquisition and integration decisions?

### Construct
Innovation stage = unrealized innovation measured by R&D intensity versus realized/protected innovation measured by patenting intensity; vertical relatedness = product-market links between potential buyers and targets.

### Data
U.S. public firms and acquisitions linked to 10-K product descriptions, BEA input-output product vocabulary, R&D, patents, and firm characteristics.

### Design/ID
Builds a text-based vertical-relatedness network between firm pairs and tests whether R&D and patenting predict vertical acquisitions; uses incentive-provision difficulty and holdup risk to test the mechanism.

### Findings
R&D-intensive firms are less likely to be acquired vertically, while firms with patented innovation are more likely to sell to vertically related buyers. The R&D effect is stronger when acquirers would have difficulty preserving innovation incentives, and the patent effect is stronger when holdup risk is higher. Vertical acquisitions occur when innovation becomes realized and protected enough that ownership can reduce holdup without destroying R&D incentives.
