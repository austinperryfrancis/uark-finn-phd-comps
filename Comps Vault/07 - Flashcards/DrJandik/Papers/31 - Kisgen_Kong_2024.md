# Kisgen & Kong (2024)

Paper: [[Kisgen_Kong_2024]]
Week: 8 - Internal capital markets and diversification

## Front
Kisgen & Kong (2024)
Cross-Subsidization in Conglomerate Firms: Evidence from Government Spending Shocks

## Back

### Research Question
Do conglomerates cross-subsidize divisions after segment-specific demand shocks, and does this improve or worsen firm performance?

### Construct
Cross-subsidization = investment spillovers across conglomerate segments; government-spending shock = change in demand for government-dependent industries.

### Data
U.S. conglomerate segments and matched standalone firms; segment-level investment and operating performance linked to federal government spending exposure.

### Design/ID
Uses changes in federal government spending as demand shocks to government-dependent segments; compares investment responses of conglomerate segments to matched standalone firms; tests whether shocks to one segment affect investment in other, less government-dependent segments within the same conglomerate.

### Findings
Government-spending shocks affect investment less for conglomerate segments than for matched standalone firms. Positive shocks to government-dependent segments increase investment in other segments within the same conglomerate, consistent with cross-subsidization. This cross-subsidization worsens operating performance and increases the diversification discount.
