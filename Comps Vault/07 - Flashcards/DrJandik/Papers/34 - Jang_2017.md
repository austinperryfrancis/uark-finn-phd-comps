# Jang (2017)

Paper: [[Jang_2017]]
Week: 9 - Internal capital markets, diversification, and M&A basics

## Front
Jang (2017)
International Corporate Diversification and Financial Flexibility

## Back

### Research Question
Does international diversification give firms greater financial flexibility by improving access to foreign capital markets?

### Construct
International corporate diversification = multinational operations; financial flexibility = ability to access foreign funding sources and maintain investment when home-country capital markets are disrupted.

### Data
U.S. multinational and domestic firms; firm financials linked to foreign bank loans, international bond issuance, and investment outcomes.

### Design/ID
Compares multinationals to domestic firms in their use of foreign bank loans and international bonds; uses the 2007–2009 financial crisis, especially the post-Lehman credit shock, to test whether multinationals substitute toward foreign funding and cut U.S. investment less than domestic firms.

### Findings
U.S. multinationals have greater access to foreign funding sources: they are more likely to borrow from foreign banks and issue international bonds. During the financial crisis, multinationals rely more on foreign funding and reduce U.S. investment less than domestic firms. International diversification increases financial flexibility by providing access to alternative capital markets.
