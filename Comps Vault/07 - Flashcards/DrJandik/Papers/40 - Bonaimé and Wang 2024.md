# Bonaime & Wang (2024)

Paper: [[Bonaimé and Wang 2024]]
Week: 10 - M&A: sources of synergies

## Front
Bonaime & Wang (2024)
Mergers, Product Prices, and Innovation: Evidence from the Pharmaceutical Industry

## Back

### Research Question
Do mergers in the pharmaceutical industry affect product prices and innovation?

### Construct
Merger effects = changes in drug prices and innovation after consolidation; innovation is separated into improvements to existing drugs versus new drug development.

### Data
Pharmaceutical mergers linked to drug-level prices, product characteristics, patents, clinical trials, labeling changes, and manufacturing changes.

### Design/ID
Compares drugs affected by mergers to similar unaffected drugs before and after merger completion; tests price changes and different types of innovation around pharmaceutical consolidation.

### Findings
Pharmaceutical mergers raise prices modestly, about 2% for affected drugs, but the effect is temporary. Mergers increase innovation related to existing products, such as labeling and manufacturing changes, but do not increase new drug development. The paper shows that M&A can create product-market effects beyond simple cost synergies, but not all “innovation” is the same.
