# Denis & McConnell (2003)

Paper: [[Denis_McConnell_2003]]
Week: 1 - Corporate governance: issues and indices

## Front
Denis & McConnell (2003)
International Corporate Governance

## Back

### Research Question
What does international corporate governance research show about how governance mechanisms differ across countries and affect firm value/manager discipline?

### Construct
Governance mechanisms = internal controls such as boards, ownership, compensation, and external controls such as takeovers and legal/investor-protection systems.

### Data
Survey of international corporate governance literature.

### Design/ID
Organizes evidence into first-generation studies of firm-level mechanisms within countries and second-generation studies of cross-country legal/institutional differences.

### Findings
Governance systems differ across countries; ownership is often concentrated outside the U.S./U.K.; legal protection and enforcement shape ownership structure, capital-market development, and effectiveness of firm-level governance.
