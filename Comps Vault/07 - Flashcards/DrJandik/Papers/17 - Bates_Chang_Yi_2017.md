# Bates et al. (2018)

## Front
Bates et al. (2018)
Why Has the Value of Cash Increased Over Time?

## Back

### Research Question
Why has the marginal value of corporate cash increased over time, even as firms hold more cash?

### Construct
Marginal value of cash = stock-market value of an additional dollar of corporate cash; also studies speed of adjustment toward target cash holdings.

### Data
U.S. public nonfinancial, nonutility firms from CRSP/Compustat, 1980–2009.

### Design/ID
Estimates marginal value of cash using Faulkender-Wang excess-return models; studies time trends across decades; relates cash value to investment opportunities, cash-flow volatility, financial constraints, credit spreads, product-market competition, diversification, sentiment, and governance; estimates cash adjustment speeds.

### Findings
The value of $1 of cash rises from about $0.61 in the 1980s to $1.04 in the 1990s and $1.12 in the 2000s. The increase is mainly tied to stronger precautionary motives: better investment opportunities, higher cash-flow volatility, credit-market risk, competition, and reduced diversification. Cash adjustment speeds decline, especially for constrained firms with cash deficits, suggesting financing frictions make cash more valuable.
