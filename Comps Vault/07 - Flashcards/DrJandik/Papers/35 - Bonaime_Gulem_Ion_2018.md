# Bonaime et al. (2018)

Paper: [[Bonaime_Gulem_Ion_2018]]
Week: 9 - Internal capital markets, diversification, and M&A basics

## Front
Bonaime et al. (2018)
Does policy uncertainty affect mergers and acquisitions?

## Back

### Research Question
Does political and regulatory policy uncertainty affect merger and acquisition activity?

### Construct
Policy uncertainty = Baker-Bloom-Davis economic policy uncertainty index and policy-category uncertainty measures; M&A activity = aggregate and firm-level merger activity.

### Data
U.S. M&A deals from SDC linked to firm characteristics, macroeconomic uncertainty measures, and policy uncertainty indexes.

### Design/ID
Regresses aggregate and firm-level M&A activity on policy uncertainty while controlling for macroeconomic uncertainty and firm/macro conditions; tests channels using less reversible deals, firms more sensitive to policy uncertainty, competition-driven deals, hedging deals, and merger contract terms.

### Findings
Policy uncertainty is strongly negatively associated with M&A activity. The effect is strongest for tax, government spending, monetary/fiscal policy, and regulatory uncertainty. The evidence supports a real-options channel: firms delay acquisitions when policy uncertainty is high, especially when deals are less reversible or firms are more policy-sensitive. Contract terms suggest policy uncertainty increases target bargaining power.
