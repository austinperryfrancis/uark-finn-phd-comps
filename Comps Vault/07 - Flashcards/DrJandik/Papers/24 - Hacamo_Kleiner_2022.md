# Hacamo & Kleiner (2022)

Paper: [[Hacamo_Kleiner_2022]]
Week: 6 - Networks in corporate finance

## Front
Hacamo & Kleiner (2022)
Competing for Talent: Firms, Managers, and Social Networks

## Back

### Research Question
Do social networks help firms recruit talented managers by reducing information frictions in the managerial labor market?

### Construct
Social network connection = MBA student’s random team/cohort connection to a former employee of a potential employer; managerial ability = GMAT scores and first-semester MBA performance.

### Data
Indiana University MBA graduates linked to random cohort/team assignments, prior employers, GMAT/class performance, LinkedIn employment histories, and survey evidence.

### Design/ID
Uses random assignment of MBA students to teams/cohorts to create exogenous connections between students and firms through former employees; estimates discrete-choice employment models; tests effects by manager ability, tie strength, geography, and firm type.

### Findings
Social networks increase firms’ likelihood of hiring high-ability managers, but do not affect hiring of low-ability managers. Effects are strongest for nonlocal firms, stronger ties, and peers living near each other. Survey evidence suggests networks work by transmitting information about firm fundamentals to potential applicants.
