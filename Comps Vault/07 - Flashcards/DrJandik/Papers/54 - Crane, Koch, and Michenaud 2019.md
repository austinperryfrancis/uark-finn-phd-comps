# Crane et al. (2019)

Paper: [[Crane, Koch, and Michenaud 2019]]
Week: 14 - Governance

## Front
Crane et al. (2019)
Institutional investor cliques and governance

## Back

### Research Question
Do networks of institutional investors affect corporate governance through coordinated voice?

### Construct
Institutional investor cliques = groups of institutions connected through common ownership patterns; governance voice = voting against management and influencing governance outcomes.

### Data
U.S. institutional ownership and proxy voting data linked to firm governance proposals, firm characteristics, liquidity shocks, and mutual fund scandal shocks.

### Design/ID
Constructs institutional investor cliques from ownership networks and tests whether clique ownership predicts coordinated voting and governance outcomes; uses the mutual fund scandal as a shock to clique ownership.

### Findings
Institutional investor cliques vote together and are more likely to oppose poor management proposals. Higher clique ownership strengthens shareholder voice and governance discipline, while reducing the importance of exit as a governance mechanism. The paper shows that institutional ownership networks matter for how investors influence firms.
