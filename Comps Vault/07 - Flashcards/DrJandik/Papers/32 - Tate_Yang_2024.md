# Tate & Yang (2024)

Paper: [[Tate_Yang_2024]]
Week: 8 - Internal capital markets and diversification

## Front
Tate & Yang (2024)
The Human Factor in Acquisitions: Cross-industry Labor Mobility and Corporate Diversification

## Back

### Research Question
Can human capital transferability create value in diversifying acquisitions?

### Construct
Human capital transferability = similarity of worker skills and occupations across industry pairs; internal labor market = ability to reallocate workers across industries inside the merged firm.

### Data
U.S. diversifying acquisitions linked to Census/LEHD employer–employee data on worker skills, occupations, labor mobility, productivity, and post-merger divestitures.

### Design/ID
Measures cross-industry labor mobility and skill overlap to identify industry pairs where human capital can be redeployed; tests whether firms are more likely to diversify into labor-related industries and whether those deals produce better labor productivity, worker retention, internal transfers, and divestiture outcomes.

### Findings
Diversifying acquisitions are more common between industries with higher human capital transferability. These acquisitions generate larger labor productivity gains, retain more high-skill workers, involve more worker movement across industries inside the merged firm, and are less likely to be later divested. Human capital reallocation helps explain value creation in some seemingly unrelated diversifying acquisitions.
