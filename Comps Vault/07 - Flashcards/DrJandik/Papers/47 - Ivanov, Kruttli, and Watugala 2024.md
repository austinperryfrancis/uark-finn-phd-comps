# Ivanov et al. (2024)

Paper: [[Ivanov, Kruttli, and Watugala 2024]]
Week: 12 - Climate finance

## Front
Ivanov et al. (2024)
Banking on Carbon: Corporate Lending and Cap-and-Trade Policy

## Back

### Research Question
How do banks adjust corporate lending when climate transition risk increases through cap-and-trade policy?

### Construct
Transition risk = exposure of greenhouse-gas-emitting firms to cap-and-trade regulation; bank credit terms include loan maturity, loan type, interest rates, and lender composition.

### Data
U.S. public and private firms’ facility-level GHG emissions linked to Federal Reserve Y-14 and Shared National Credit loan data around California and Waxman-Markey cap-and-trade policies.

### Design/ID
Uses two policy settings: California’s cap-and-trade bill, exploiting variation in firms’ California emissions exposure; and Waxman-Markey, exploiting the free-permit threshold for energy-intensive manufacturers.

### Findings
Banks respond quickly to transition risk by shortening maturities, reducing term-loan exposure, shifting firms toward credit lines, charging higher rates, and involving more shadow banks. Effects are concentrated among private firms, while public firms are much less affected.
