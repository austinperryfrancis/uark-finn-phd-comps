# Djankov et al. (2008)

Paper: [[Djankov_2008]]
Week: 1 - Corporate governance: issues and indices

## Front
Djankov et al. (2008)
The Law and Economics of Self-Dealing

## Back

### Research Question
How do legal rules protecting minority shareholders from insider self-dealing affect financial market development?

### Construct
Anti-self-dealing index = country-level measure of legal protection against related-party transactions/tunneling, based mainly on approval, disclosure, and private litigation rights.

### Data
72 countries; Lex Mundi law-firm responses to a stylized self-dealing transaction; country-level stock market outcomes.

### Design/ID
Constructs legal index from rules governing a hypothetical conflicted transaction; relates index to stock market capitalization, listed firms, IPOs, ownership concentration, and control premia across countries.

### Findings
Stronger anti-self-dealing protections predict better stock market development and lower private benefits of control; the index performs better than older anti-director-rights measures.
