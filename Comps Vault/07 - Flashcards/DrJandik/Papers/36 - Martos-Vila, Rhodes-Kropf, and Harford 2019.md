# Martos-Vila et al. (2019)

Paper: [[Martos-Vila, Rhodes-Kropf, and Harford 2019]]
Week: 9 - Internal capital markets, diversification, and M&A basics

## Front
Martos-Vila et al. (2019)
Financial versus Strategic Buyers

## Back

### Research Question
Why does M&A activity shift between financial buyers, such as private equity firms, and strategic buyers, such as operating companies?

### Construct
Buyer type = financial versus strategic acquirer; debt misvaluation = debt-market conditions that make leverage cheaper or more expensive than fundamentals imply; project coinsurance/governance differences = reasons strategic and financial buyers value the same target differently.

### Data
U.S. merger and acquisition data with buyer-type classifications, deal characteristics, debt-market conditions, and measures of debt misvaluation.

### Design/ID
Develops a model in which debt misvaluation interacts with acquirer type, governance, and project coinsurance; tests whether debt overvaluation predicts shifts between financial-buyer and strategic-buyer M&A activity.

### Findings
Debt-market misvaluation helps explain shifts between financial and strategic buyers. Overvalued/cheap debt favors financial buyers because LBOs rely heavily on debt and PE governance helps control moral hazard. Strategic buyers differ because they combine targets with existing projects and have coinsurance/synergy motives.
