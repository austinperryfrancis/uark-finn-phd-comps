# Bharath & Hertzel (2019)

Paper: [[Bharath_Hertzel_2019]]
Week: 3 - Capital structure: bankruptcy, product markets, debt ownership, and debt maturity

## Front
Bharath & Hertzel (2019)
External Governance and Debt Structure

## Back

### Research Question
Do firms substitute between creditor governance and other external governance mechanisms when choosing bank loans versus public debt?

### Construct
External governance pressure = product-market competition and takeover-market discipline; debt structure = choice between bank loans and public debt, plus covenant strictness.

### Data
U.S. public firms issuing new debt; bank loan and public bond data linked to firm characteristics, import tariff changes, BC laws, and loan covenant data.

### Design/ID
Tests debt-source choice using quasi-exogenous governance shocks: tariff reductions increase product-market competition; state antitakeover BC laws reduce takeover pressure. Also examines covenant strictness around these shocks.

### Findings
Stronger external governance lowers demand for bank governance: tariff reductions make firms less likely to use bank debt and loosen covenants. Weaker takeover governance makes firms more likely to use bank debt and tightens covenants. Firms substitute across governance mechanisms.
