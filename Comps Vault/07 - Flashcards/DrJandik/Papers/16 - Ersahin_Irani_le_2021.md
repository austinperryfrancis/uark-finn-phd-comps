# Ersahin et al. (2021)

Paper: [[Ersahin_Irani_le_2021]]
Week: 4 - Debt contracts: covenants, social capital, lender hold-up, and creditor control

## Front
Ersahin et al. (2021)
Creditor control rights and resource allocation within firms

## Back

### Research Question
What operational changes occur inside firms after covenant violations shift control rights to creditors?

### Construct
Creditor control rights = control transferred to lenders after debt covenant violations; resource allocation = employment, investment, and establishment closures across firm units.

### Data
U.S. public firms linked to Census establishment-level data, Compustat, covenant-violation data from Nini et al. (2012), and Dealscan lender data.

### Design/ID
Compares violating firms to similar nonviolating firms; studies within-firm changes across core/peripheral, productive/unproductive, risky, and CEO-linked establishments; uses covenant-threshold/RDD-style tests and lender industry-experience tests.

### Findings
Covenant violations lead firms to cut employment and investment and close establishments, especially in peripheral, less productive, risky, and agency-cost-prone units. Effects are stronger when relationship lenders have industry expertise. Creditor control can improve resource allocation by disciplining inefficient internal investment.
