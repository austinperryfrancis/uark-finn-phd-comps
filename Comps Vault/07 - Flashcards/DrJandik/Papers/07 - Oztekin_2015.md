# Oztekin (2015)

Paper: [[Oztekin_2015]]
Week: 2 - Capital structure: determinants and targets, taxes, and information asymmetry

## Front
Oztekin (2015)
Capital Structure Decisions around the World: Which Factors Are Reliably Important?

## Back

### Research Question
Which firm, industry, macro, and institutional factors reliably determine capital structure across countries?

### Construct
Reliable leverage determinants and institutional quality; leverage measured using book/market debt ratios and adjustment speed toward target leverage.

### Data
International firm sample from 37 countries; firm financials linked to country-level legal, financial, and institutional characteristics.

### Design/ID
Dynamic panel/partial-adjustment leverage models; tests which determinants are consistently significant across countries and institutional settings; two-step tests relating institutions to leverage levels and adjustment speeds.

### Findings
Reliable determinants are size, tangibility, industry leverage, profitability, and inflation. Better institutions lead to faster adjustment toward target leverage; stronger creditor protection/effective bankruptcy procedures are associated with higher leverage.
