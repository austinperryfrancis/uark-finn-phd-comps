# Gormley et al. (2023)

Paper: [[Gormley, Gupta, Matsa, Mortal, and Yang 2023]]
Week: 15 - Governance

## Front
Gormley et al. (2023)
The Big Three and Board Gender Diversity: The Effectiveness of Shareholder Voice

## Back

### Research Question
Can large institutional investors use shareholder voice to change board composition?

### Construct
Shareholder voice = Big Three campaigns to increase board gender diversity; board diversity = female representation on corporate boards.

### Data
U.S. public firms linked to Big Three ownership, board gender composition, director characteristics, voting outcomes, and governance data.

### Design/ID
Uses the Big Three’s board-diversity campaigns as a shock to investor voice and tests whether targeted firms add female directors, change search criteria, and promote women into board leadership roles.

### Findings
Big Three campaigns significantly increased female board representation. Firms responded by broadening director searches beyond traditional networks, appointing women with less conventional executive backgrounds, and promoting women into key board roles. The paper shows that coordinated shareholder voice by large index investors can meaningfully affect governance.
