# Prilmeier (2017)

Paper: [[Prilmeier_2017]]
Week: 4 - Debt contracts: covenants, social capital, lender hold-up, and creditor control

## Front
Prilmeier (2017)
Why do loans contain covenants? Evidence from lending relationships

## Back

### Research Question
How do lending relationships affect covenant use, and do covenants change as relationship lenders learn about borrowers?

### Construct
Covenant tightness = average ex ante violation probability of financial covenants; covenant intensity = number of financial covenants; relationship lending = relationship duration and relationship intensity.

### Data
U.S. corporate loans from DealScan linked to Compustat; large loans to public borrowers.

### Design/ID
Regresses covenant tightness/intensity on lender relationship measures; separates duration from intensity; tests opaque borrowers; uses IV based on borrower proximity to active syndicating banks, plus matching/robustness tests.

### Findings
Covenant tightness decreases over the duration of a lending relationship, especially for opaque borrowers, consistent with lenders relaxing covenants as information asymmetry falls. Covenant intensity follows an inverted-U shape in relationship intensity: loans have the most covenants in intermediate relationships and fewer covenants with weak or exclusive relationships.
