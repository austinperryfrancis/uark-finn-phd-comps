# Jenter & Lewellen (2021)

Paper: [[Jenter and Lewellen 2021]]
Week: 14 - Governance

## Front
Jenter & Lewellen (2021)
Performance-Induced CEO Turnover

## Back

### Research Question
How much CEO turnover is caused by poor firm performance, and what does this imply about boards’ monitoring of CEOs?

### Construct
Performance-induced CEO turnover = turnover that would not have occurred if firm performance had been good.

### Data
U.S. public firms with CEO turnover, firm performance, CEO characteristics, and governance data.

### Design/ID
Estimates how CEO turnover probability responds to firm performance and uses the model to separate performance-induced turnover from other turnover.

### Findings
A large share of CEO turnover is performance-induced, even though only some turnover is labeled forced. Boards respond strongly to poor performance, but learning about CEO ability appears slow and noisy. The paper shows that CEO turnover-performance sensitivity is broader than forced-turnover classifications suggest.
