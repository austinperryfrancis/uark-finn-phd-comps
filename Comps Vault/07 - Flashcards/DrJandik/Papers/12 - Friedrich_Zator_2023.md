# Friedrich & Zator (2023)

Paper: [[Friedrich_Zator_2023]]
Week: 3 - Capital structure: bankruptcy, product markets, debt ownership, and debt maturity

## Front
Friedrich & Zator (2023)
Flexibility costs of debt: Danish exporters during the cartoon crisis

## Back

### Research Question
Does high leverage reduce firms’ ability to adapt to negative product-market demand shocks?

### Construct
Financial flexibility = low leverage before the shock; demand shock = exposure to the 2006 boycott of Danish products in Muslim countries after the cartoon crisis.

### Data
Danish exporters; administrative firm-level trade, financial-statement, employer–employee, product-sales, and loan-level data.

### Design/ID
DiD comparing firms exposed to Muslim markets with other exporters before/after the boycott; triple-difference tests compare low- vs. high-leverage exposed firms; uses debt-maturity variation and controls for confounders.

### Findings
Exposed firms lose the majority of exports to Muslim countries. Low-leverage firms redirect sales to new and existing non-Muslim product-destination markets and offset losses; high-leverage firms do not enter new markets and instead downsize employment/costs. Debt reduces flexibility in crisis.
