# Kahle & Stulz (2021)

Paper: [[Kahle_Stulz_2021]]
Week: 5 - Cash and payouts: cash, dividends, and repurchases

## Front
Kahle & Stulz (2021)
Why are corporate payouts so high in the 2000s?

## Back

### Research Question
Why are U.S. corporate payouts much higher in the 2000s, and are high payouts crowding out investment?

### Construct
Payouts = dividends plus net share repurchases; payout rate = payouts relative to operating income; free cash flow and firm lifecycle characteristics help explain payout capacity.

### Data
U.S. public industrial firms from Compustat/CRSP, 1971–2019.

### Design/ID
Decomposes the rise in aggregate payouts into higher income versus higher payout rates; estimates payout-rate models using pre-2000 firm characteristics and tests whether they predict 2000s payouts; compares payers, nonpayers, and top payout firms.

### Findings
Average annual real payouts are more than three times higher in 2000–2019 than before 2000. Higher aggregate income explains about 37% of the increase, while higher payout rates explain about 63%. The rise is driven mainly by repurchases and by firms being older, larger, more cash-rich, and having more free cash flow. Capital expenditures fall similarly for payers and nonpayers, so high payouts do not appear to be the main cause of lower investment.
