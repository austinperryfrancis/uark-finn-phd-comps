# DeAngelo & Roll (2015)

Paper: [[DeAngelo_Roll_2015]]
Week: 2 - Capital structure: determinants and targets, taxes, and information asymmetry

## Front
DeAngelo & Roll (2015)
How Stable Are Corporate Capital Structures?

## Back

### Research Question
Are corporate capital structures actually stable over long horizons, as implied by persistent leverage/target-leverage views?

### Construct
Capital-structure stability = persistence of firm leverage rankings and leverage ratios over time; uses book and market leverage.

### Data
U.S. industrial firms; long-run leverage data from annual reports, Moody’s, Compustat, and CRSP.

### Design/ID
Tracks leverage cross-sections and firm leverage paths over time; examines cross-section similarity, leverage quartile migration, stable leverage regimes, industry medians, and simulations of target-leverage models.

### Findings
Leverage stability is the exception, not the rule. Cross-sectional similarity fades over 5–10 years; many firms move across leverage quartiles; high leverage is usually temporary; stable regimes occur mostly at low leverage. Target-leverage models fit the data only when they put little weight on maintaining a specific ratio.
