# Dey et al. (2024)

Paper: [[Dey, Starkweather, and White 2024]]
Week: 15 - Governance

## Front
Dey et al. (2024)
Proxy Advisory Firms and Corporate Shareholder Engagement

## Back

### Research Question
Do proxy advisory firms affect firms’ shareholder engagement behavior?

### Construct
Proxy-advisor pressure = ISS scrutiny after weak say-on-pay support; shareholder engagement = firms’ meetings and communication with institutional investors.

### Data
U.S. public firms’ say-on-pay votes, ISS recommendations, shareholder engagement disclosures, institutional ownership, director voting outcomes, and governance characteristics.

### Design/ID
Uses the ISS 70% say-on-pay threshold as a quasi-natural experiment: firms just below the threshold receive heightened ISS scrutiny, allowing comparison to firms just above the threshold.

### Findings
Firms facing ISS scrutiny increase both the likelihood and intensity of shareholder engagement, especially when agency conflicts and director voting risk are high. The effects persist, and shareholders appear to value the engagement, showing that proxy advisors can shape governance through firm-investor communication, not just voting recommendations.
