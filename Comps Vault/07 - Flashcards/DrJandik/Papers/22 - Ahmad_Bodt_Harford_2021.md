# Ahmad et al. (2021)

Paper: [[Ahmad_Bodt_Harford_2021]]
Week: 6 - Networks in corporate finance

## Front
Ahmad et al. (2021)
International Trade and the Propagation of Merger Waves

## Back

### Research Question
Do merger waves propagate across countries through international trade links?

### Construct
Trade network = country and country-industry import/export links; merger-wave exposure = trade-weighted M&A activity in connected trading-partner countries.

### Data
Global country- and industry-level trade data linked to domestic and cross-border mergers from SDC.

### Design/ID
Builds yearly global trade networks and tests whether trade-weighted merger activity in connected countries predicts a country’s own merger-wave activity; uses tariff cuts and trade sanctions as instruments for trade flows; includes placebo tests with randomly shuffled trade links.

### Findings
Merger waves spread across borders through trade networks. M&A activity in trading-partner countries predicts both cross-border and domestic merger activity in the subject country. The evidence supports a learning/exposure channel: trade makes firms more informed about and exposed to foreign markets, so merger waves in trade-linked countries can trigger related merger activity at home.
