# Biermann & Huber (2024)

Paper: [[Biermann_Huber_2024]]
Week: 8 - Internal capital markets and diversification

## Front
Biermann & Huber (2024)
Tracing the International Transmission of a Crisis through Multinational Firms

## Back

### Research Question
Do multinational firms transmit financial shocks across countries through internal capital markets?

### Construct
Shock transmission = financial distress passed from German parent firms to foreign affiliates; internal capital markets = intra-firm lending between parents and affiliates; credit shock = lending cut to German parents.

### Data
German multinational firms and their foreign affiliates; regulatory data on parent-affiliate internal capital positions linked to bank relationships, firm financials, sales, and employment.

### Design/ID
Uses a credit supply shock from Commerzbank’s lending cut to German parent firms; compares foreign affiliates of affected versus unaffected parents; tests whether exposed affiliates increased internal lending to parents, became financially constrained, and experienced lower real growth.

### Findings
Multinationals transmit financial shocks internationally through internal capital markets. Foreign affiliates support distressed German parents through internal lending, become financially constrained themselves, and experience lower sales and employment growth. Effects are stronger for weaker foreign affiliates, weaker when affiliates have better access to local credit markets, and larger for financial shocks than nonfinancial shocks.
