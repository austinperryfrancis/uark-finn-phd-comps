# Hasan et al. (2017)

Paper: [[Hasan_Hoi_2017]]
Week: 4 - Debt contracts: covenants, social capital, lender hold-up, and creditor control

## Front
Hasan et al. (2017)
Social Capital and Debt Contracting: Evidence from Bank Loans and Public Bonds

## Back

### Research Question
Does local social capital affect debt contracting by reducing lenders’ concerns about opportunistic borrower behavior?

### Construct
Social capital = county-level cooperative norms and social networks; measured using voter turnout, census response rates, nonprofit organizations, and social associations.

### Data
U.S. public firms; county-level social capital data linked to bank loans, public bonds, firm characteristics, and headquarters locations.

### Design/ID
Regresses loan spreads, nonprice loan terms, bond spreads, and debt choice on county social capital; uses organ donation as an alternative measure and headquarters relocations that increase/decrease social capital in DiD tests.

### Findings
Firms in high-social-capital counties receive lower bank loan spreads, looser collateral/covenant requirements, lower bond spreads, and are more likely to use public bonds over bank loans. Debt holders view social capital as an external discipline that reduces borrower opportunism.
