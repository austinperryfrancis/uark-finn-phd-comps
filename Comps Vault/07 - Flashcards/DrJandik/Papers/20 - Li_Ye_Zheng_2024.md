# Li et al. (2024)

Paper: [[Li_Ye_Zheng_2024]]
Week: 5 - Cash and payouts: cash, dividends, and repurchases

## Front
Li et al. (2024)
Price ceilings, market structure, and payout policies

## Back

### Research Question
Why have share repurchases risen relative to dividends, and how do market-structure frictions affect firms’ ability to repurchase shares?

### Construct
Repurchase frictions = SEC Rule 10b-18 price ceilings plus market-structure limits on issuers’ ability to buy at those prices; payout policy = repurchases versus dividends.

### Data
U.S. public firms’ repurchases/dividends linked to market-structure reforms, Rule 10b-18 constraints, and the Tick Size Pilot Program.

### Design/ID
Uses the 2016 Tick Size Pilot as a controlled experiment; also studies historical market-structure reforms including Manning Rule, Order Handling Rules, tick-size reductions, decimalization, autoquotes, and dark-pool access.

### Findings
Market-structure reforms made it easier for firms to buy shares under Rule 10b-18 price ceilings, increasing repurchases over time. The Tick Size Pilot reversed some reforms and reduced repurchases, especially for tick-constrained firms. Price ceilings and market-structure frictions explain about 18% of the secular increase in repurchases and help explain why repurchases have not fully crowded out dividends.
