# Graham et al. (2017)

Paper: [[Graham_2017]]
Week: 2 - Capital structure: determinants and targets, taxes, and information asymmetry

## Front
Graham et al. (2017)
Tax Rates and Corporate Decision-making

## Back

### Research Question
What tax rates do firms actually use in corporate decisions, and does using the wrong tax-rate input lead to suboptimal capital structure and investment decisions?

### Construct
Tax-rate input choice = whether firms use marginal tax rates, statutory tax rates, GAAP effective tax rates, or jurisdiction-specific tax rates in decisions.

### Data
Survey of corporate tax executives linked to Compustat financials and estimated marginal tax rates.

### Design/ID
Compares firms using ETRs to firms using MTRs/STRs; tests whether decision errors are larger when the gap between a firm’s ETR and estimated MTR is larger.

### Findings
Many firms use GAAP ETRs or statutory tax rates; few use marginal tax rates. Firms using ETRs make less efficient capital-structure and investment decisions when ETR differs from MTR; average capital-structure inefficiency costs about $16 million.
