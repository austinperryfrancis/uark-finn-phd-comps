# Faulkender et al. (2019)

Paper: [[Faulkender_Hankins_Petersen_2019]]
Week: 5 - Cash and payouts: cash, dividends, and repurchases

## Front
Faulkender et al. (2019)
Understanding the Rise in Corporate Cash: Precautionary Savings or Foreign Taxes

## Back

### Research Question
Is the rise in U.S. corporate cash driven mainly by precautionary savings motives or by foreign tax incentives?

### Construct
Cash location = domestic versus foreign cash; foreign tax incentive = low foreign tax rates and unrepatriated foreign income; income shifting = related-party foreign sales enabled by intangible assets/IP.

### Data
U.S. multinational firms; nonpublic BEA foreign-affiliate data linked to Compustat, 1998–2008.

### Design/ID
Separates total cash into domestic and foreign cash; tests whether precautionary variables versus foreign tax rates explain each; examines firms with R&D/intangible assets and related-party foreign sales.

### Findings
The rise in cash is concentrated in foreign subsidiaries of multinationals. Precautionary motives explain domestic cash, but not the foreign cash buildup. Falling foreign tax rates and income shifting explain most foreign cash growth, especially for firms with intellectual property and related-party foreign sales.
