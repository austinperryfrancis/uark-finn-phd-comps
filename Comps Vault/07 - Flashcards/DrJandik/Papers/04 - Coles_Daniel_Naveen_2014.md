# Coles et al. (2014)

Paper: [[Coles_Daniel_Naveen_2014]]
Week: 1 - Corporate governance: issues and indices

## Front
Coles et al. (2014)
Co-opted Boards

## Back

### Research Question
Does formal board independence overstate monitoring quality if CEO-appointed directors are less independent in practice?

### Construct
Co-option = fraction of directors appointed after CEO assumed office. Non-co-opted independence = independent directors already on board before CEO.

### Data
U.S. public firms; board/director data linked to CEO turnover, pay, PPS, performance, and investment.

### Design/ID
Panel regressions controlling for formal independence; firm FE; tenure-weighted and residual co-option; post-SOX exchange-rule DiD.

### Findings
More co-option → weaker turnover-performance sensitivity, higher CEO pay without higher PPS, and more investment. Non-co-opted independence → stronger monitoring.
