# Bris et al. (2006)

Paper: [[Bris_Welch_Zhu_2006]]
Week: 3 - Capital structure: bankruptcy, product markets, debt ownership, and debt maturity

## Front
Bris et al. (2006)
The Costs of Bankruptcy: Chapter 7 Liquidation versus Chapter 11 Reorganization

## Back

### Research Question
How costly are corporate bankruptcies, and how do Chapter 7 liquidations compare with Chapter 11 reorganizations?

### Construct
Bankruptcy costs = direct expenses, asset value changes during bankruptcy, time in bankruptcy, creditor recovery rates, and APR violations.

### Data
Corporate bankruptcies in Arizona and the Southern District of New York federal bankruptcy courts, 1995–2001; includes both Chapter 7 and Chapter 11 cases, public and private firms.

### Design/ID
Hand-coded bankruptcy court documents; compares Chapter 7 vs. Chapter 11 outcomes while controlling for endogenous self-selection into bankruptcy procedure.

### Findings
Bankruptcy costs are highly heterogeneous and measurement-sensitive. Chapter 7 is not faster or cheaper than Chapter 11 in direct expenses; Chapter 11 appears to preserve assets better and allows creditors to recover more. Bankruptcy professionals often capture much of the remaining value in Chapter 7.
