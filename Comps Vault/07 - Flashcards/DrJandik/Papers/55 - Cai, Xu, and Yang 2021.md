# Cai et al. (2021)

Paper: [[Cai, Xu, and Yang 2021]]
Week: 14 - Governance

## Front
Cai et al. (2021)
Paying by Donating: Corporate Donations Affiliated with Independent Directors

## Back

### Research Question
Do firms use charitable donations connected to independent directors to influence board monitoring?

### Construct
Affiliated donation = corporate donation to a charity connected to an independent director; director independence/monitoring = ability to discipline CEO pay and turnover.

### Data
U.S. public firms’ corporate charitable donations linked to director charity affiliations, CEO compensation, CEO turnover, firm performance, and governance characteristics.

### Design/ID
Tests whether affiliated donations are associated with weaker monitoring; uses forced college football coach turnovers as shocks to directors’ charity affiliations to address endogenous donation choices.

### Findings
Affiliated donations are common and often undisclosed. Firms making donations tied to independent directors pay CEOs more and show weaker CEO turnover-performance sensitivity. The paper shows that “independent” directors can be compromised through indirect private benefits.
