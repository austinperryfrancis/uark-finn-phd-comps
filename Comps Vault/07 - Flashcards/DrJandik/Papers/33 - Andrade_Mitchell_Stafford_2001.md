# Andrade et al. (2001)

Paper: [[Andrade_Mitchell_Stafford_2001]]
Week: 9 - Internal capital markets, diversification, and M&A basics

## Front
Andrade et al. (2001)
New Evidence and Perspectives on Mergers

## Back

### Research Question
What are the main empirical patterns in merger activity, and what do they imply about why mergers occur and whether they create value?

### Construct
Merger waves = clustering of M&A activity over time and by industry; merger gains = announcement returns and post-merger operating performance.

### Data
U.S. mergers and acquisitions; deal characteristics, announcement returns, and post-merger operating performance.

### Design/ID
Survey-style empirical overview of merger patterns; documents industry clustering, deregulation-driven merger waves, payment-method changes, hostile-versus-friendly deal trends, combined announcement returns, and post-merger operating performance relative to industry peers.

### Findings
Merger activity clusters strongly by industry, with deregulation explaining a large share of 1990s merger activity. Compared with the 1980s, 1990s mergers are mostly stock swaps and hostile takeovers largely disappear. Combined announcement returns are positive and post-merger operating performance improves relative to peers, suggesting mergers create value on average, even though gains are distributed unevenly between targets and acquirers.
