# El-Khatib et al. (2015)

Paper: [[Khatib_Fogel_Jandik_2015]]
Week: 6 - Networks in corporate finance

## Front
El-Khatib et al. (2015)
CEO network centrality and merger performance

## Back

### Research Question
Does a CEO’s position in the social network affect acquisition behavior and merger performance?

### Construct
CEO network centrality = CEO’s position/power in the executive-director social network; measured using closeness, degree, betweenness, and eigenvector centrality.

### Data
S&P 1500 acquirers and U.S. public targets from SDC; CEO network data from BoardEx linked to CRSP, Compustat, RiskMetrics, and governance data.

### Design/ID
Regresses acquisition frequency, bidder CARs, and combined deal synergies on CEO centrality; controls for firm, CEO, deal, and governance characteristics; tests whether internal governance, the market for corporate control, and the executive labor market discipline high-centrality CEOs.

### Findings
High-centrality CEOs make more acquisitions, but their deals have lower bidder returns and lower combined synergies. They are less disciplined by takeover and CEO labor markets, and internal governance only partly limits their acquisition activity. Results suggest network centrality gives CEOs power/influence that can increase entrenchment and private benefits rather than improve deal quality.
