# Santioni et al. (2020)

Paper: [[Santioni_Schiantarelli_Strahan_2020]]
Week: 8 - Internal capital markets and diversification

## Front
Santioni et al. (2020)
Internal Capital Markets in Times of Crisis: The Benefit of Group Affiliation

## Back

### Research Question
Do internal capital markets help business-group-affiliated firms survive when external finance becomes distressed?

### Construct
Group affiliation = membership in a business group; internal capital market = intra-group cash/resource transfers; external finance distress = deterioration in firms’ banks’ health.

### Data
Italian firms and business groups; firm financials, group affiliation, intra-group financial flows, and firm-bank relationships from Italian administrative/credit-register data.

### Design/ID
Compares survival and performance of group-affiliated versus unaffiliated firms during the global financial/euro crises; tests whether group-wide cash flow and bank distress predict survival and intra-group transfers; examines whether funds move from cash-rich to cash-poor or high-opportunity group firms.

### Findings
Group-affiliated firms survive crises better than unaffiliated firms. Internal capital markets become more valuable when banks are distressed: transfers increase when external finance tightens, with funds moving from cash-rich to cash-poor firms and sometimes toward firms with better investment opportunities. Group affiliation helps substitute for impaired external capital markets.
