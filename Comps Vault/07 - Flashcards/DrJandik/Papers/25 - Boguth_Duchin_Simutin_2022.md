# Boguth et al. (2022)

Paper: [[Boguth_Duchin_Simutin_2022]]
Week: 7 - Networks, internal capital markets, and diversification

## Front
Boguth et al. (2022)
Dissecting Conglomerate Valuations

## Back

### Research Question
How can we value conglomerate divisions without relying on standalone firms, and what do those divisional valuations reveal about internal capital markets and diversification?

### Construct
Divisional Q = conglomerate-implied Tobin’s Q for a division; standalone Q = valuation of focused firms in the same industry; internal capital markets = capital reallocation across divisions inside conglomerates.

### Data
U.S. conglomerates and business segments from Compustat segment data linked to firm-level accounting and valuation data.

### Design/ID
Develops a method that estimates divisional Qs from conglomerates themselves by regressing conglomerate valuation multiples on divisional industry exposure weights; compares divisional Qs to standalone-firm Qs; studies credit shocks, division covariance, investment-Q sensitivity, spinoffs, and diversifying acquisitions.

### Findings
Divisional Qs differ systematically from standalone-firm Qs and are less sensitive to economic and credit shocks, consistent with conglomerate divisions being insulated by internal capital markets and coinsurance. Conglomerate investment is more sensitive to divisional Qs than standalone Qs, and divisional-standalone Q differences help explain internal capital allocation and diversifying acquisitions.
