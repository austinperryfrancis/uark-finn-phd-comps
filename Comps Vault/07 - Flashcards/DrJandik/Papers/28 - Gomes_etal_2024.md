# Gomes et al. (2024)

Paper: [[Gomes_etal_2024]]
Week: 7 - Networks, internal capital markets, and diversification

## Front
Gomes et al. (2024)
Analyst Coverage Networks and Corporate Financial Policies

## Back

### Research Question
Do analyst coverage networks transmit peer effects in firms’ financial policies, especially capital structure and security issuance decisions?

### Construct
Analyst peer network = firms connected by common sell-side analyst coverage; peer effects = firms responding to analyst peers’ leverage, debt issuance, and equity issuance choices.

### Data
U.S. public firms with analyst coverage linked to leverage, debt issuance, equity issuance, stock-return shocks, brokerage closures, and analyst characteristics.

### Design/ID
Uses analyst coverage networks and a “friends-of-friends” approach to separate endogenous peer effects from peer characteristics; uses idiosyncratic equity shocks to analyst peers and brokerage closures/mergers as shocks to common coverage.

### Findings
Firms’ leverage and issuance decisions respond to the financial policies of analyst-network peers, and these effects are distinct from industry peer effects. Peer effects are stronger when common analysts are more experienced or influential and weaker after brokerage closures reduce common coverage. Analysts help transmit financial-policy information across firms.
