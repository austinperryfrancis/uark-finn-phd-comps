# Lee et al. (2018)

Paper: [[Lee, Mauer, and Xu 2018]]
Week: 10 - M&A: sources of synergies

## Front
Lee et al. (2018)
Human capital relatedness and mergers and acquisitions

## Back

### Research Question
Does human capital relatedness between acquirers and targets help explain merger likelihood and merger value creation?

### Construct
Human capital relatedness = similarity between firms’ occupational human-capital profiles; product-market relatedness is used to separate labor-related synergies from product-market overlap.

### Data
U.S. domestic mergers and acquisitions from SDC linked to firm financials, announcement returns, employment, wages, operating performance, and asset-sale data.

### Design/ID
Constructs firm-pair human-capital relatedness and tests whether it predicts merger likelihood, merger returns, post-merger performance, labor outcomes, and asset-sale returns when labor does versus does not transfer.

### Findings
Firms are more likely to merge, and merger gains are larger, when their human capital is related. The effect is strongest when firms are not already product-market related. Employment and wage reductions after high-relatedness mergers, plus asset-sale tests, support human capital as a source of M&A synergies.
