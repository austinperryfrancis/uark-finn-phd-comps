# Kogan et al. (2017)

Paper: [[Kogan, Papanikolaou, Seru, and Stoffman 2017]]
Week: 13 - Innovation and machine learning

## Front
Kogan et al. (2017)
Technological Innovation, Resource Allocation, and Growth

## Back

### Research Question
How can researchers measure the economic value of innovation, and does valuable innovation affect firm growth, resource allocation, and aggregate growth?

### Construct
Innovation value = stock-market reaction to patent grants; private value of a patent is inferred from the firm’s market-value change when the patent is granted.

### Data
U.S. patents linked to firm stock returns, firm outcomes, citations, and aggregate productivity/growth measures.

### Design/ID
Uses patent-grant announcement returns to construct a market-based measure of patent value, then tests whether valuable patents predict firm growth, creative destruction, reallocation, and aggregate productivity.

### Findings
Market-based patent values predict firm growth, reallocation, and creative destruction better than simple patent counts or citations alone. Valuable innovation is associated with medium-run aggregate growth and productivity, making patent-value measures useful for studying how innovation affects firms and the economy.
