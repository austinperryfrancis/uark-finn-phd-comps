# Barrot & Sauvagnat (2016)

Paper: [[Barrot and Sauvagnat 2016]]
Week: 11 - Suppliers and customers

## Front
Barrot & Sauvagnat (2016)
Input Specificity and the Propagation of Idiosyncratic Shocks in Production Networks

## Back

### Research Question
Do firm-level shocks propagate through supplier-customer networks, and is propagation stronger when inputs are difficult to substitute?

### Construct
Input specificity = how hard it is for customers to replace a supplier’s input; supply-chain shock = natural disasters hitting supplier locations.

### Data
U.S. supplier-customer relationships linked to firm financials, stock returns, supplier locations, and major natural disasters.

### Design/ID
Uses natural disasters as plausibly exogenous shocks to suppliers and tests whether affected suppliers’ customers experience worse performance, especially when supplier inputs are more specific.

### Findings
Idiosyncratic supplier shocks propagate to customers: customers of disaster-hit suppliers experience lower sales growth and negative stock-market reactions. The effect is stronger when inputs are more specific and harder to replace, showing that production networks transmit firm-level shocks through input dependence.
