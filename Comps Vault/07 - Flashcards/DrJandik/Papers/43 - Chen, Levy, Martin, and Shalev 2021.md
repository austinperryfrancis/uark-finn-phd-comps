# Chen et al. (2021)

Paper: [[Chen, Levy, Martin, and Shalev 2021]]
Week: 11 - Suppliers and customers

## Front
Chen et al. (2021)
Buying products from whom you know: personal connections and information asymmetry in supply chain relationships

## Back

### Research Question
Do personal connections between buyers and suppliers affect supplier selection and contracting when information asymmetry is high?

### Construct
Buyer-supplier personal connection = shared professional, educational, or social ties between executives/directors at customer and supplier firms; information asymmetry = difficulty assessing supplier quality.

### Data
U.S. customer-supplier relationships linked to executive/director connection data, supplier characteristics, contract terms, and relationship outcomes.

### Design/ID
Tests whether connected suppliers are more likely to be chosen, especially when information asymmetry is high; examines contract restrictions and ex post relationship performance to distinguish information benefits from favoritism.

### Findings
Buyers are more likely to select personally connected suppliers, especially when supplier information asymmetry is high. Connected relationships use less restrictive contracts and perform better, suggesting that personal ties reduce information frictions in supplier selection rather than simply reflecting cronyism.
