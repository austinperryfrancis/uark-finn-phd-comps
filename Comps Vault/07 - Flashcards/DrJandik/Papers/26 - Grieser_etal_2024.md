# Grieser et al. (2022)

Paper: [[Grieser_etal_2024]]
Week: 7 - Networks, internal capital markets, and diversification

## Front
Grieser et al. (2022)
Network effects in corporate financial policies

## Back

### Research Question
Do firms’ capital-structure choices causally respond to the leverage choices of product-market peers, and how large are those peer effects?

### Construct
Peer effects in leverage = strategic complementarity in firms’ capital-structure choices; product-market peer network = Hoberg-Phillips text-based product-similarity network.

### Data
U.S. public firms; financial policy data linked to Hoberg-Phillips product-market peer networks.

### Design/ID
Uses a spatial-econometrics framework that exploits heterogeneous and intransitive peer networks to identify structural peer-effect coefficients; uses exogenous leverage shifters such as idiosyncratic equity shocks and debt coming due.

### Findings
Firms’ leverage choices exhibit significant but moderate peer effects: a firm’s leverage responds positively to peer leverage, with estimated coefficients around 0.20. Prior estimates likely overstated the magnitude. Evidence supports leverage as a strategic corporate policy shaped by product-market peers.
