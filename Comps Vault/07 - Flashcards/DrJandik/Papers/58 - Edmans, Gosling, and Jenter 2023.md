# Edmans et al. (2023)

Paper: [[Edmans, Gosling, and Jenter 2023]]
Week: 15 - Governance

## Front
Edmans et al. (2023)
CEO compensation: Evidence from the field

## Back

### Research Question
How do directors and investors think CEO compensation is actually set, and what constraints matter beyond standard incentive-contracting theory?

### Construct
CEO compensation constraints = participation, incentives, fairness concerns, public controversy, shareholder pressure, and nonmonetary CEO motivation.

### Data
Surveys of corporate directors and institutional investors about CEO pay-setting practices, constraints, incentives, and governance views.

### Design/ID
Field survey comparing directors’ and investors’ views on CEO compensation, including what motivates CEOs, why pay levels are high, and what limits boards’ pay-setting choices.

### Findings
CEO pay is shaped by more than participation and incentive concerns. Directors report strong constraints from fairness, controversy, and shareholder reactions, and many view intrinsic motivation and reputation as more important CEO motivators than incentive pay. The paper shows that real-world CEO compensation reflects political, social, and governance constraints in addition to optimal contracting.
