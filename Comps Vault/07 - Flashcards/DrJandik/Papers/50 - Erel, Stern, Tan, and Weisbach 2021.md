# Erel et al. (2021)

Paper: [[Erel, Stern, Tan, and Weisbach 2021]]
Week: 13 - Innovation and machine learning

## Front
Erel et al. (2021)
Selecting Directors Using Machine Learning

## Back

### Research Question
Can machine learning help identify directors who are likely to underperform, and what does that reveal about board selection?

### Construct
Director quality = predicted director performance using director, board, and firm characteristics; performance is measured mainly by shareholder voting support after appointment.

### Data
U.S. director appointments linked to director characteristics, board/firm characteristics, shareholder voting outcomes, and governance measures.

### Design/ID
Trains machine-learning models to predict director performance and compares predictably good versus predictably bad directors; then studies which firms appoint predictably bad directors.

### Findings
Machine learning can identify predictably bad directors, who receive lower shareholder support and are more likely to be male, overboarded, and highly networked. Weak-governance firms are more likely to appoint them, suggesting director selection reflects governance frictions rather than lack of available information.
