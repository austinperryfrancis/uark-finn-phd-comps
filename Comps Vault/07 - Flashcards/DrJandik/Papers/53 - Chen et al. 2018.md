# Chen et al. (2018)

Paper: [[Chen et al. 2018]]
Week: 14 - Governance

## Front
Chen et al. (2018)
Clarity Begins at Home: Internal Information Asymmetry and External Communication Quality

## Back

### Research Question
Does internal information asymmetry inside the firm affect the quality of external communication with investors?

### Construct
Internal information asymmetry = information gap between divisional managers and top managers, measured using differences in insider trading profits.

### Data
U.S. conglomerates with divisional and top-manager insider trading data linked to management forecasts, restatements, and firm characteristics.

### Design/ID
Uses differences in divisional versus top-manager insider trading profits to proxy for internal information asymmetry, then tests whether higher internal asymmetry predicts lower-quality external disclosure.

### Findings
Firms with higher internal information asymmetry issue less accurate, less specific, less frequent, and more pessimistic forecasts and have more error-driven restatements. The paper shows that external disclosure quality depends partly on how well information flows inside the firm.
