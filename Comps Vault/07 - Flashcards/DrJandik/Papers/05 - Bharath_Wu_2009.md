# Bharath et al. (2009)

Paper: [[Bharath_Wu_2009]]
Week: 2 - Capital structure: determinants and targets, taxes, and information asymmetry

## Front
Bharath et al. (2009)
Does Asymmetric Information Drive Capital Structure Decisions?

## Back

### Research Question
Does asymmetric information help explain firms’ capital-structure decisions, as predicted by the pecking order theory?

### Construct
Information asymmetry index based on market microstructure measures of adverse selection; also uses financing deficit and debt issuance behavior.

### Data
U.S. firms; firm financials linked to market-based adverse-selection measures.

### Design/ID
Sorts firms by adverse-selection deciles and tests whether debt issuance responds more strongly to financing deficits when information asymmetry is high; also uses panel regressions of leverage levels/changes controlling for standard leverage determinants.

### Findings
Firms with higher adverse selection rely more on debt to cover financing deficits; highest adverse-selection firms issue about 30 cents more debt per dollar of financing deficit than lowest-adverse-selection firms. Asymmetric information matters for capital structure, but is not the only determinant; pecking order works best when adverse selection is severe.
