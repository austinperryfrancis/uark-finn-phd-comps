# Week 14 - Governance

## Front
What is the Week 14 narrative on governance through information, investors, boards, and CEO turnover?

## Back

### Narrative
This section returns to governance with more specific monitoring channels. Chen et al. show that internal information asymmetry affects the clarity of external communication, linking internal systems to disclosure quality. Crane et al. show institutional investor cliques can coordinate governance pressure through shared holdings and networks. Cai et al. show firms may use donations affiliated with independent directors, raising concerns about director independence and influence. Jenter and Lewellen show CEO turnover is strongly tied to performance, but the sensitivity and interpretation of turnover require careful measurement.

### Main Findings
- Poor internal information environments reduce external communication quality.
- Institutional investor networks can amplify governance voice.
- Affiliated charitable donations can compromise or influence independent directors.
- CEO turnover responds to poor performance, revealing board monitoring intensity.

### Exam Use
Use this week to show that governance operates through information, investor coordination, board incentives, and executive replacement rather than only formal board structure.
