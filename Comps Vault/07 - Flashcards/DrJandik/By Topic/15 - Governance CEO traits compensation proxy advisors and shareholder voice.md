# Week 15 - Governance

## Front
How does Week 15 extend governance to CEO traits, compensation, shareholder voice, and proxy advisors?

## Back

### Narrative
This section focuses on modern governance frictions around executives and external monitors. Kaplan et al. show that common CEO overconfidence proxies may capture broader traits and ability, not just overoptimism, which matters for interpreting behavioral corporate finance evidence. Edmans et al. bring field evidence on CEO compensation, emphasizing practical constraints and institutional features that standard incentive models miss. Gormley et al. show large index investors can use voice to increase board gender diversity. Dey et al. show proxy advisory firms affect shareholder engagement, indicating that governance intermediaries can influence firm behavior before votes occur.

### Main Findings
- Longholder-style overconfidence proxies can capture traits and ability beyond pure overconfidence.
- CEO pay is shaped by market constraints, retention, benchmarking, and institutional practice.
- Big Three voice campaigns can change board diversity outcomes.
- Proxy advisor scrutiny changes firm engagement with shareholders.

### Exam Use
Use this week to discuss governance measurement and intermediation. CEO traits, pay, index-investor voice, and proxy advisors all shape governance outside the classic board-independence channel.
