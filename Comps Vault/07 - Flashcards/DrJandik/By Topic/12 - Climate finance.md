# Week 12 - Climate finance

## Front
What is the Week 12 narrative on measuring and pricing climate risk in corporate finance?

## Back

### Narrative
This section shows climate risk entering corporate finance through measurement, supply chains, lending, and adaptation. Hall et al. show that firms can face climate exposure through suppliers even when their own emissions appear low. Sautner et al. provide a text-based measure of firm-level climate exposure and show it relates to real outcomes and market prices. Ivanov et al. show banks adjust lending when cap-and-trade policy raises transition risk. Pankratz and Schiller show physical climate exposure affects supply-chain relationships and that customers adapt by changing suppliers.

### Main Findings
- Supply-chain climate exposure can be material even for low-emission firms.
- Textual climate exposure measures capture firm-level risks that matter for outcomes and prices.
- Climate transition policy changes bank lending to exposed firms.
- Physical climate risk affects supplier relationships and customer adaptation.

### Exam Use
Use this week to separate physical risk, transition risk, measurement, and network exposure. Climate finance questions often hinge on where the exposure enters the firm.
