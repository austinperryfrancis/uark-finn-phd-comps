# Week 10 - M&A: sources of synergies

## Front
What is the Week 10 narrative on where merger synergies come from?

## Back

### Narrative
This section decomposes synergy into labor, innovation, management, pricing, and product-market channels. Lee et al. show that human capital relatedness makes mergers more likely and more valuable because workers and skills can be redeployed. Fresard et al. show vertical acquisitions are tied to the stage of innovation, with integration more useful when innovation needs complementary assets or coordination. Bai et al. show that acquirers can create value by transferring stronger management practices to targets. Bonaime and Wang show that pharmaceutical mergers affect prices and innovation, emphasizing product-market consequences beyond announcement returns.

### Main Findings
- Human capital relatedness predicts merger likelihood and post-merger value creation.
- Innovation stage affects whether vertical integration through acquisition is valuable.
- Better management practices can be transferred from acquirers to targets.
- Pharmaceutical mergers can change product prices and innovation incentives.

### Exam Use
Use this week to move beyond generic synergy. Good merger answers should identify the specific asset or friction being combined: labor, innovation, management, pricing power, or coordination.
