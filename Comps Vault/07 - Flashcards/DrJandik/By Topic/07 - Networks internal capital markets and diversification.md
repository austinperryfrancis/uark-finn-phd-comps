# Week 7 - Networks, internal capital markets, and diversification

## Front
How does Week 7 connect networks, conglomerates, peer effects, and internal capital markets?

## Back

### Narrative
This section moves from external networks to internal and peer networks. Boguth et al. show that conglomerate valuation depends on how internal capital markets allocate resources across segments. Grieser et al. show that financial policies exhibit network effects, so firms respond to peers and connected firms rather than choosing policies in isolation. Massa et al. add an international asset-pricing angle by showing that strategic business groups can affect risk and valuation across connected firms. Gomes et al. show analyst coverage networks can transmit information and shape corporate financial policies.

### Main Findings
- Conglomerate discounts and premia depend on internal allocation, not diversification alone.
- Corporate financial policies respond to network peers.
- Business groups create strategic links that matter for international asset pricing.
- Analyst networks affect information production and financial-policy choices.

### Exam Use
Use this week to discuss firms as connected systems. Internal and external networks shape capital allocation, valuation, and policy diffusion.
