# Week 13 - Innovation and machine learning

## Front
How does Week 13 use innovation and machine learning to measure intangible corporate outcomes?

## Back

### Narrative
This section focuses on measuring hard-to-observe intangible assets and decision quality. Kogan et al. use patent-market information to measure the private and social value of innovation and connect valuable innovation to growth and resource allocation. Erel et al. use machine learning to predict director performance, showing that board selection contains predictable inefficiencies. Li et al. use machine learning on firm language to measure corporate culture and connect it to behavior and performance. Babina et al. measure AI investment through hiring and worker exposure, linking AI to firm growth and product innovation.

### Main Findings
- Market-based patent value predicts firm growth and contributes to aggregate growth.
- Machine learning can identify directors likely to underperform.
- Text-based culture measures predict firm behavior and outcomes.
- AI investment is associated with growth and product innovation.

### Exam Use
Use this week to discuss measurement innovation. ML is useful when it turns text, patents, resumes, or labor data into firm-level constructs that can be tied to outcomes.
