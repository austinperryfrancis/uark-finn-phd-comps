# Week 9 - Internal capital markets, diversification, and M&A basics

## Front
How does Week 9 bridge diversification and the basic economics of mergers?

## Back

### Narrative
This section turns internal capital market logic into merger motives. Andrade et al. establish the empirical baseline: mergers occur in waves, cluster by industry, and are often tied to shocks such as deregulation. Jang shows that international diversification can provide financial flexibility by opening access to foreign capital markets. Bonaime et al. show policy uncertainty delays M&A, consistent with real-options logic. Martos-Vila et al. show that debt-market conditions help determine whether financial or strategic buyers dominate merger activity.

### Main Findings
- Merger waves cluster by industry and often follow economic or regulatory shocks.
- Multinationals have greater foreign funding access and preserve investment better during domestic credit stress.
- Policy uncertainty reduces M&A activity, especially for less reversible deals.
- Cheap or overvalued debt shifts activity toward financial buyers, especially private equity.

### Exam Use
Use this week to explain merger waves as responses to shocks, financing conditions, uncertainty, and buyer-specific advantages.
