# Week 6 - Networks in corporate finance

## Front
What is the Week 6 narrative on why networks matter in corporate finance?

## Back

### Narrative
This section treats networks as channels for information, influence, talent, and shocks. El-Khatib et al. show that CEO network centrality can matter for merger performance, but networks can create both access and agency problems. Ahmad et al. show that international trade networks transmit merger waves across countries and industries. Cai et al. show director appointments depend on social connections, highlighting who-you-know effects in governance labor markets. Hacamo and Kleiner extend the logic to managerial labor markets, showing firms compete for talent through social networks.

### Main Findings
- CEO centrality affects M&A outcomes through access, influence, and potential agency costs.
- Merger waves propagate through international trade links.
- Director appointments are strongly shaped by personal and professional connections.
- Managerial social networks affect talent competition and firm hiring outcomes.

### Exam Use
Use this week to argue that corporate decisions are embedded in networks. Networks move information and resources, but they can also transmit shocks and distort selection.
