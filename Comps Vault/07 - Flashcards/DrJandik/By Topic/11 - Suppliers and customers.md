# Week 11 - Suppliers and customers

## Front
How does Week 11 describe supplier-customer relationships as transmission channels?

## Back

### Narrative
This section treats supply chains as contractual and informational networks. Barrot and Sauvagnat show that idiosyncratic firm shocks propagate more strongly when inputs are specific and hard to replace. Intintoli et al. show that CEO turnover at major customers disrupts supplier relationships and harms suppliers. Chen et al. show personal connections reduce information asymmetry in buyer-supplier matching and contracting. Dai et al. show high-CSR customers influence supplier CSR, so supply chains transmit governance and social preferences as well as shocks.

### Main Findings
- Input specificity makes customer shocks propagate to suppliers.
- Customer CEO turnover can disrupt supplier performance and relationships.
- Personal connections affect supplier selection and contracting under information asymmetry.
- Customer CSR pressure improves supplier CSR behavior.

### Exam Use
Use this week to argue that supply chains are not just production links. They transmit shocks, information, monitoring, and stakeholder preferences.
