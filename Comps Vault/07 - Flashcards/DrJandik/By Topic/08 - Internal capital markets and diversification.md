# Week 8 - Internal capital markets and diversification

## Front
What is the Week 8 narrative on when internal capital markets create value?

## Back

### Narrative
This section asks when diversified firms and groups allocate capital better than external markets. Santioni et al. show that group affiliation can provide financing benefits during crises by moving capital internally. Biermann and Huber show that multinational firms transmit financial shocks internationally through internal lending, so internal capital markets can spread both support and constraints. Kisgen and Kong show conglomerates cross-subsidize divisions after demand shocks, with investment spillovers across segments. Tate and Yang show diversification can create value when human capital is transferable across industries after acquisitions.

### Main Findings
- Business groups can relax financing constraints during crises.
- Multinationals transmit shocks across borders through internal capital markets.
- Conglomerates cross-subsidize divisions in response to segment-level demand shocks.
- Diversifying acquisitions create more value when human capital can move across industries.

### Exam Use
Use this week to give a balanced internal-capital-market answer: internal markets can insure, reallocate, and redeploy talent, but they can also transmit shocks and misallocate resources.
